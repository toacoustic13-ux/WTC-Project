Culture & Traditions

Khmer Traditions

Khmer traditions and culture are rich and deeply connected to history, religion, family values, and respect for elders. Cambodian people follow many customs that have been passed down from generation to generation. Religion, especially Buddhism, plays an important role in Khmer daily life. People often visit temples, offer food to monks, and celebrate religious festivals. Traditional festivals such as Khmer New Year and Pchum Ben bring families together to show respect to ancestors and enjoy cultural activities. Khmer culture also includes traditional arts such as classical dance, music, and crafts. Traditional clothing, especially the sampot, reflects the beauty and identity of Khmer people. Khmer food, with dishes like amok and kuy teav, is another important part of the country’s cultural heritage. Respect, kindness, hospitality, and strong family relationships are central values in Khmer society. By preserving these traditions, Cambodians maintain their unique identity and pass their culture on to future generations.

Cambodia is also famous for its many ancient temples, which represent the greatness of the Khmer Empire and the creativity of Khmer architecture. Temples such as Angkor Wat are important symbols of Cambodian history, culture, and national identity.
